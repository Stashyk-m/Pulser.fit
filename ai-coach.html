DeepTech · Our own IP

A proprietary movement interpretation layer, not a wrapper.

Most "AI fitness" apps are a thin UI on top of someone else's API. The AI Coach inside Pulser.One is built on our own proprietary movement interpretation layer — pose estimation, biomechanical modelling, injury-risk scoring, periodisation. It runs on-device wherever it can, in the cloud where it needs to scale.

That means three things: privacy-first by design, a moat that compounds with every session trained on the platform, and features a SaaS CRM cannot retrofit in a sprint.

Our model

Not OpenAI. Not a third-party API. Movement, risk and plan generation run on our own stack.

On-device where possible

Pose estimation and form scoring run locally on your phone. Low latency, high privacy.

Peer-reviewed basis

Injury-risk and programming models trace back to published biomechanics research.

Data flywheel

Every session across Pulser.One, Pulser.Gym and Pulser.Coach sharpens the model.

Data Sources

Three signals. One brain.

📷 Camera

Real-time form analysis

MediaPipe-based pose estimation runs on the member's smartphone. Frame-by-frame form feedback on squat, deadlift, bench — no special hardware required.

⌚ Wearables

HR, HRV, recovery, load

Apple Watch, Garmin, Whoop, Oura feed continuous cardiovascular and recovery signals. The coach knows when to push and when to pull back.

🧬 Health records

User-shared history

Members can share injuries, chronic conditions, medications. Plans are tailored around real medical context — with full user control and EHDS compliance.

What It Does

From rep quality to retention prediction.

Real-time form feedback

Point your phone. The coach scores depth, bar path, hip drive, knee tracking. Haptic or audio cues tell you what to fix — before you load another plate.

Personalised plans

Weekly programming that adapts to what actually happened: missed sessions, poor recovery, tough life days. Not a static PDF.

Preventive health alerts

Combined signals flag elevated injury risk, signs of overtraining, cardiovascular anomalies. Members are nudged — gyms get context.

Retention & engagement scoring

For gym operators: a daily list of members at churn risk, ranked — with the reason why and the suggested intervention.
